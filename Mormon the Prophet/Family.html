Mormon the Prophet

In 1823 a young boy of age 17 had many visits from an Angel of the Lord. This was something that the boy was accustomed too after his first vision in 1820. The boy was named Joseph Smith Jr. and he "saw a pillar of light exactly over my head, above the brightness of the sun, which descended gradually until it fell upon me. “… When the light rested upon me I saw two Personages, whose brightness and glory defy all description, standing above me in the air. One of them spake unto me, calling me by name and said, pointing to the other—This is My Beloved Son. Hear Him!” (Joseph Smith—History 1:16–17)."

This first vision was very important as well as the 1823 angel visitation because it led way to the translation of the Book of Mormon.

The Book of Mormon contains the word of god, and the gospel of Jesus Christ. They are told through first hand accounts of ancient prophet, seers, and revelators. These prophets include Nephi, Alma, Moroni, etc.

However, there was one prophet among them that has contributed to the Book of Mormon, not in terms of prophetic visions or stories of baptisms. This prophet was named Mormon, from what the book is titled.

According to the intorduction of the Book of Mormon, the prophet Mormon was not only a prophet but a historian. He was able to help keep records and redacted a couple of things in the scriptures. He was able to abridge the many years of ancient Nephite and Lamanite history and was able to give to us the important things that the Lord wanted the people of today to learn. "3 And now, I speak somewhat concerning that which I have written; for after I had made an abridgment from the plates of Nephi, down to the reign of this king Benjamin, of whom Amaleki spake, I searched among the records which had been delivered into my hands, and I found these plates, which contained this small account of the prophets, from Jacob down to the reign of this king Benjamin, and also many of the words of Nephi"(Words of Mormon 1:3).
